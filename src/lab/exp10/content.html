Theory

Back To Experiment List

 

Vapour pressure is the pressure of a vapour that is in equilibrium with its liquid phase in a closed system. The vapour pressure of a liquid is the indication of a liquid’s evaporation rate.

 

A common relation between the vapour pressure and temperature is the Antoine equation. Using this equation, the vapour pressure of a species can be calculated given the temperature and vice versa. This is very useful in chemical industries, since operations like distillation require us to know the vapour pressure of the various components as the operating temperature.

 

In this experiment, we determined the parameters of the Antoine equation for the given component and also its heat of vapourization.

 

In this experiment, we aim to determine the vapour pressure at various temperatures for a pure substance and to calculate the correlation constant in the vapour pressure equation. We will also determine the latent heat of vaporization of the substance.

Procedure

Back To Experiment List

 

1) Sample of the liquid is filled in ebulliometer.

 

2) Make the initial pressure atmospheric by opening the pressure valve and then closing it again.

 

3) Start the heater and temperature sensor.

 

4) Vapour form and rise in the tube to the flask chamber.

 

5) Vapour moves through thermo wall and then condenses.

 

6) Allow the system to reach steady state.

 

7) Observe the drop rate and adjust the rheostat such that the drop rate should be between 40 and 90 per minute.

 

8) Take two reading with slight change in the rheostat.

 

9) Change the pressure using vacuum pump and again do the same procedure.

 

10) Take atleast six readings.
